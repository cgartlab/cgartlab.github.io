## What is Data Sovereignty

Since I started using the internet in college, I've never trusted any cloud storage or cloud services. This feeling faded a lot after the development of mobile internet because the internet was everywhere. But after AI emerged, I truly realized how valuable personal data is. At the same time, I also think this is something that most people using these AI platforms easily overlook.

This happened just half a month ago, during the Chinese New Year holiday when I finally had some free time. I wanted to set up an OpenClaw assistant on my NAS to experience it. But training this assistant would require spending time chatting with it and writing mental documents, which was very annoying. I thought to myself, I've already had countless conversations with various AI models, so why not just export those chat records and feed them to OpenClaw?

As it turned out, only Claude, Gemini, ChatGPT, and DeepSeek have the actual function to export chat data in bulk, while mainstream domestic models like Doubao, Qianwen, and Kimi only support exporting single conversations (as of the time of writing this article). I was curious and even asked Doubao's expert mode about this, and its response was roughly as follows. For a detailed explanation, you can refer to [this link](https://www.doubao.com/thread/we1b42e217234488e).

![Why Doubao Can't Export Data](../_images/13%20玄光周刊-你和AI聊过的天，可能并不属于你-1772120071317.webp)

What does this mean? At least in China, in today's era of increasingly large centralized platforms, your data is being stored on someone else's servers. Let's put aside what enterprises might do with this data for now, but the data you generate after using their products only nominally belongs to you. I also asked OpenClaw to send several agents to conduct a comprehensive verification and comparison, and we compiled a table.

![Comparison of Data Export Capabilities of Mainstream Large Models](../_images/13%20玄光周刊-你和AI聊过的天，可能并不属于你-1772118775750.webp)

Therefore, I am more convinced that building your own home data center is absolutely necessary in the AI era, especially gradually transforming various data and information services into self-hosted solutions. Self-hosting is not just a technical choice; it is actually the externalization of your values regarding personal data.

The three biggest benefits of doing this are:

1. **Data Control**: Services may shut down, policies may change, but self-hosting ensures that your data is always in your own hands
2. **Privacy Protection**: Your data is no longer someone else's asset, nor will it be used as feed for AI models
3. **Customization**: Your tools should serve your needs

Of course, there are corresponding costs: you need to spend a lot of time self-learning, you also need to have the energy to maintain devices and systems, and finally, you have to bear the risks yourself. **But this entire process is precisely the true meaning of "sovereignty"—the equality of rights and responsibilities.**

When you entrust your data to a platform, you are actually purchasing a "responsibility outsourcing" service. The platform bears the operational risks, while you cede your data sovereignty and privacy. Self-hosting means you actively take back the responsibility. The learning, maintenance, and risks you undertake are exactly the "price" you pay to redeem your sovereignty.

Companies may close down, platforms may go bankrupt, but the data and skills you master will always belong to you.

### Times New American - System Design Behind Fonts

🔗[https://hsu.cy/2025/12/times-new-american/](https://hsu.cy/2025/12/times-new-american/ "https://hsu.cy/2025/12/times-new-american/")

In December 2025, US Secretary of State Rubio requested that State Department documents be changed from Calibri back to Times New Roman.

The Times New Roman font is widely mistaken as a symbol of "tradition" and "authority," but this is actually the result of historical accidents and systemic inertia, which exposes the lack of technical standards for government documents.

Writers care about fonts, designers care about systems. Politicians seek presence, while designers should focus on underlying logic: ensuring that documents can still be accurately read in twenty years is far more important than using serif or sans serif today. Tools will change, but data is eternal.
