## This Issue's Theme: Creative Tools in the AI Era

### When "Empowerment" Becomes Standard

Open any creative tool's website, and the homepage is almost always dominated by "AI Empowerment."

Before 2024, "AI-powered" was a selling point. In 2026, **not having AI is the news**.

This isn't a complaint. What I want to say is: beneath all the hype, there's a question that never disappeared—Are AI tools expanding the boundaries of creation, or flattening them? Let's talk about this this issue.

### The Inversion of Barriers

Over the past decade, creator communities have repeatedly discussed "democratization of tools." Democratization refers to **lowering the economic and technical barriers of professional creative tools, enabling more people to access and use these tools, thereby gaining the ability to express and create.**

Photoshop is too expensive—we have GIMP. After Effects is too complex—we have DaVinci Resolve. Tool普及 = barrier lowering = more opportunities to create. I've always认同 this logic.

**Until AI image generation tools appeared.**

You don't need to master sketching, lighting, color theory—just describe your idea in natural language, and the model outputs a "professional"-looking image.

The problem is: when anyone can generate a "good-looking" image, **what makes something "good"** becomes even more acute.

Aesthetic judgment, thematic conception, narrative structure—these skills that cannot be replaced by prompts have become **scarce goods** in the AI era.

My observation: **the cost of tool democratization is the systematic transfer of skills.** Traditional manual skills depreciate, while new meta-skills (describing, judging, filtering) appreciate. Entry became easier, but mastery became different—and not in the same direction.

### The Battle of Two Paths

Observing the current market landscape of AI creative tools reveals a clear division.

**Path A: Professionalization**

Represented by Runway. Gen-4/Gen-4.5 continue to deepen cinema-grade video generation capabilities, from shot control to style consistency, constantly moving closer to professional production workflows.

The target user is professional teams and independent creators willing to pay for quality.

**Path B: Generalization**

Represented by Leonardo AI. After being acquired by Canva for $320 million, the positioning becomes clearer: not replacing professional tools, but becoming a "one-click upgrade" entry point for the mass creative market.

The advantage lies not in peak quality, but in deep integration with design workflows.

**These two paths have no right or wrong, but they point to radically different creative futures.**

- Professionalization → AI becomes a new member in the professional toolbox
- Generalization → AI reshapes the entire creative consumer market, "everyone is a creator" transforms from slogan to reality

The real question is not which path will win, but: **How do creators find their position between the two paths?**

### Open Source: The Overlooked Force

Commercial platform marketing noise is so loud that open-source model voices are often overlooked.

But if we seriously look at the impact of Stable Diffusion and its derivative models, open-source communities' shaping of the AI creative ecosystem may be more far-reaching than any single commercial product.

**The core value of open-source models lies not in being free, but in being auditable, modifiable, and customizable.** When a creator can run a model locally, a team can fine-tune their own dedicated model, a community can jointly maintain a model free from commercial company意志, the initiative of creation truly returns to creators.

Of course, the cost of open-source is higher technical barriers and more distributed user experience. But this precisely echoes the paradox mentioned at the beginning: the tension between tool democratization and technical understanding barriers is most fully expressed in the open-source domain.

### Authenticity: An Unavoidable Problem

Finally, let me discuss a topic that makes me uncomfortable but must be faced: the authenticity of AI-generated content.

Deepfakes have made "seeing is believing" history. AI-generated images, videos, and texts are flooding the internet at an exponential rate, blurring the boundaries between reality and fiction.

**Creators no longer face just "how to create," but also "how to prove I created this."**

Content provenance technology (C2PA, digital watermarks) is the technical response. But the real question is:

When AI can execute most of the work, what exactly does "authenticity" of creation refer to?

- Is it the freshness of technique?
- Is it the originality of ideas?
- Or is it the irreplaceable human judgment in the创作 process?

These questions have no standard answers. But practitioners who take creation seriously are answering it in their own ways.

### My Interim Conclusion

Writing to the end, I realize this weekly isn't actually about AI tools—it's about **how to stay clear-headed as a creator in the era of tool explosion.**

Tools will change, models will iterate, but some questions won't change:

**Do you think you're the master of tools, or a user of tools? What are you actually doing?**

**Do you view AI as a software? Employee? Partner? Or personal assistant?**

**When AI can easily imitate any style, how do you define and defend the "uniqueness" or "authenticity" of your work?**

**Do you have your own "evaluate-adopt" framework for the endless stream of new tools? Or are you more driven by trends and hype?**

**If all basic execution work is done by AI in the future, where do you think the core of "creation" will ultimately rest? Concepts, aesthetics, critical thinking, or something else?**

These questions are much more important than choosing Runway or Leonardo AI. Welcome to share in comments below~

### Cinema 4D 2026.2 Update: Fabric Brush and Redshift Night Sky

![I'm still on 2025.3](../_images/AI时代的创意工具%20-%20No.16%20玄光周刊-1776614518361.webp)

Link: <https://digitalproduction.com/2026/04/15/maxon-ships-cinema-4d-2026-2-update/>

C4D 2026.2's most important new feature this time is **Fabric Brush**—a modeling tool that uses the simulation system to directly shape soft materials. No rig setup needed—you can directly push and pull in the viewport to generate folds and draping, suitable for quickly creating fabric-like shapes.

The Redshift renderer also updated to 2026.5.0, adding a night sky mode—sunset to night scene lighting can now be completed directly within the Sun and Sky system, no need to piece together lighting tricks anymore.

Additionally, Maxon announced at NAB 2026 that C4D iPad beta is coming soon—this is the first time professional 3D software has come to mobile. For someone like me who has done desktop work for over a decade, this change is a bit微妙: barriers are indeed lowering, but the definition of "professional" is also being rewritten.

### Unity 6.4 Release: Scene View Grid Snapping + Unity Studio No-Code 3D Tool

![](../_images/AI时代的创意工具%20-%20No.16%20玄光周刊-1776614666180.webp)

Link: <https://www.cgchannel.com/2026/03/unity-releases-unity-6-4-and-unity-studio/>

Unity 6.4 has several noteworthy improvements for CG artists.

**The grid and snapping system** now supports custom rotation—when bridges, corridors, and other elements in the scene aren't perpendicular to the orthogonal grid, placing and aligning becomes much more convenient. Terrain material editing and Mesh LOD preview also got workflow optimizations.

More noteworthy is the release of **Unity Studio**—a completely browser-based no-code 3D content creation tool. Not for game developers, but for designers, product teams, training scenarios, used for creating 3D demos and configurators without writing a single line of code.

For those used to doing real-time rendering with Unity, Unity Studio is a dimension-reduction attack; for those not using Unity, it demonstrates that "professional tool democratization" is also happening in the real-time 3D domain.

### How Blender Makes Money: Open-Source 3D Software Business Model Analysis

![643](../_images/AI时代的创意工具%20-%20No.16%20玄光周刊-1776614691465.webp)

Link: <https://breakevenpointcalculator.com/how-does-blender-make-money-business-model-explained/>

An interesting question: Blender is completely free and open-source, how does it survive?

This article sorts through Blender's revenue structure: Blender Institute (Dutch non-profit) gets funding through subscription models, certified training, and sponsorships. In 2022, they received an $800,000 donation from Epic Games, and in 2024, another large sponsorship from Google.

Open-source doesn't mean no money to make—it just doesn't make money from software licensing. Blender's business model proves that "open source" and "sustainable business" can coexist. For creators thinking about commercializing their products, this case is more valuable than most business plan templates.

### The Four-Wheel Revolution in the PKM Track: Why No Product Actually Solved the Knowledge Management Problem

![](../_images/AI时代的创意工具%20-%20No.16%20玄光周刊-1776614798169.webp)

Link: <https://simonaking.com/blog/pkm/>

This article's conclusion is harsh: In the past six years, hundreds of products have emerged in the PKM (Personal Knowledge Management) track, raising billions in funding, but almost none actually solved the problem that "managing knowledge is more tiring than using knowledge."

The author divides PKM into four generations: Bookmark era (saving ≠ reading), Bidirectional linking era (Roam's community heat covered product flaws), AI enhancement era (turning knowledge management into a feature rather than the essence), and Karpathy's LLM Wiki era (letting AI do knowledge circulation instead of providing better tools for people). **Karpathy's solution's essence is kicking people out of the knowledge management cycle**—letting AI handle organization and connection, while people only need to ask and use. This logic is opposite to all existing PKM products.

### Obsidian + AI Plugins: Building a Local-First Second Brain

![The website style is also interesting](../_images/AI时代的创意工具%20-%20No.16%20玄光周刊-1776614874369.webp)

Link: <https://aieii.com/posts/2026-03-27-obsidian-ai/>

I've used Obsidian for 5 years without switching, for one reason only: it's one of the few tools that lets you truly own your data (I've been paying more and more attention to data sovereignty).

In 2026, the explosion of AI plugins upgrades this advantage. Take Smart Connections as an example: it's not keyword search, but semantic search—when you write "distributed collaboration," it can find the note from three months ago about "remote team management," even though the two terms are completely different.

More importantly, **local-first means your note content doesn't need to be uploaded to cloud AI for processing.** With Ollama local models, your entire knowledge base can be understood by AI while offline. This finds a balance between data security and workflow efficiency that most tools don't have.

Also, if you're tired of the cycle "saved notes can't be found, found notes can't be used," this workflow is worth seriously researching.

### Cloudflare Mesh: Cross-Region Networking Service, Supporting AI Agents to Securely Access Internal Resources

![The cyber Bodhisattva strikes again](../_images/AI时代的创意工具%20-%20No.16%20玄光周刊-1776614919671.webp)

Link: <https://blog.cloudflare.com/mesh/>

Cloudflare released Mesh private network service, providing private resource access capabilities for AI agents, developers, and remote devices. Supports 50 nodes and 50 users for free use, establishes bidirectional many-to-many network connections through a single lightweight connector, and integrates with Workers VPC, enabling agents deployed on Cloudflare Workers to directly access private databases and internal APIs.

Compared to traditional Tunnel's one-way proxy model, Mesh supports devices and nodes within the network to directly interact via private IPs. Cloudflare also plans to add hostname routing, Mesh DNS, and identity-aware routing within the year, giving agents, devices, and users independent identity labels for more granular access control.

For creator teams with private deployment needs, this service is worth following—it makes AI agents accessing internal resources simpler, not more dangerous.
