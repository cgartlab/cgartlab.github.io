## This Issue's Theme: Large Models Have Added Long-Term Memory Capabilities

In 2026, large language model platforms almost simultaneously made a key choice: adding 'complex tasks' and 'long-term memory' features. Each platform may have different names for them, and some have limited free quotas. This is the inevitable evolution of AI.

When AI starts remembering every word you've said, every choice you've made, every preference you've shown, it is no longer a passive Q&A machine, but gradually becomes your 'second brain'. It will understand your creative habits, thinking patterns, and even subconscious preferences better than you do.

**But this brings a fundamental question**: When AI understands you better than you understand yourself, is this an opportunity, or the price of privacy?

In my opinion, the key lies in—**actively defining 'who I am'**. You cannot let AI shape you through passive observation, but must shape it through active interaction. Every choice, feedback, and persistence you make is writing yourself into this 'second brain'.

### My Strategy

**First, for web applications, I create a new 'project' called 'Long-Term Memory'.** This project will have customized prompts for it, and you can also organize your publicly available information (professional fields, skilled areas, aesthetic preferences, creative habits, values, etc.) into a document and upload it.

Below is the prompt I currently use. If you like, you can directly modify it and use it yourself.

~~~markdown
You are responsible for automatically analyzing and understanding information about my characteristics from conversations and writing them into your long-term memory.
Role Setting and Objectives:
* You are an assistant with 'long-term memory' management capabilities, focusing on deep analysis, organization, and compilation of personal characteristics, preferences, and background information about the user.
* Your core task is to ensure key information from conversations is structured and persisted, so it can provide highly personalized services in future interactions.
Code of Conduct and Functions:
1) Information Extraction and Integration:
   a) Automatically analyze each conversation, extracting user behavior patterns, personality traits, interests, and important facts mentioned.
   b) Regularly (every few conversations) proactively confirm recorded information with the user and ask if there's anything to add or correct.
2) External Material Research:
   a) Before each organization and update of long-term memory, be sure to visit cgartlab.com to check if the user has published new articles or updates. Integrate insights, technical discussions, or life snippets from these articles into the memory bank.
3) Memory Bank Maintenance:
   a) Maintain the logic and organization of memory content. Avoid duplicate information and ensure seamless connection between old and new information.
   b) Ensure all records are based on facts or user's explicitly expressed intentions.
Language Style and Word Count Requirements:
* Adopt an extremely professional, rigorous, and detailed documentary narrative style.
* For each major memory update, output content should cover deep analysis, with length as detailed as possible to meet the high-quality, long-form document requirement of 'no less than 10,000 Chinese characters' (Note: In actual interaction, single replies are limited by tokens, so should be accumulated through segments or continuous updates to reach this scale).
* Use clear headings, lists, and paragraphs to organize content, making it suitable as long-term reference material.
~~~

**Then, I collect everyday trivial conversations into a project called 'Inbox'.** As the name suggests, this tells the large model that anything can be discussed here. The great benefit of doing this is that in the future, with just one command or export, you can remove the rough and keep the refined. If there are dozens or hundreds of sessions in the conversation list, I haven't yet seen any platform that can support automatic organization of sessions.

![Qwen Chat](../_images/14%20玄光周刊 - 当%20AI%20开始记住人的一切，这是我的使用策略 -1774001083736.webp)

**Finally, there's session granularity.** As shown in the figure above, besides 'Long-Term Memory' and 'Inbox', I also separately open projects like GitHub, Ollama, CSS Styles, etc.

These things being independent projects means that around them I have many ideas, or many questions that need AI to assist me in completing or answering. So how to determine whether a session in Inbox needs its own project is the distinction of session granularity.

If this is hard to understand, you can simply think of it as Weibo's #topic tag function, where each project can be seen as a topic.

## HP Eliteboard G1a Keyboard Host Form Factor Thinking

![HP Eliteboard G1a](../_images/14%20玄光周刊-当%20AI%20开始记住你的一切-1773999327236.webp)

🔗 https://www.ithome.com/0/910/806.htm

As computing power gradually shifts to the cloud, separating computing and display components can have higher freedom. I think this is very likely a form of computing device that will exist for a long time in the future.

The essence of a laptop is stuffing the computing power body into a keyboard. Separating computing and display components can have higher freedom. Wherever you go, as long as there's a monitor, projector, TV, or even a tablet, you can go out with just a keyboard. If you need a mouse for more precise pointer interaction, I think the ThinkPad TrackPoint solution is very suitable for this kind of product, further balancing functionality and portability.

The contradiction I see is that what competes with this device won't be mini PCs, but rather tablets. For keyboard hosts to have a place, computing power needs to generally exceed iPads before people would be willing to use them to connect to larger screens. It cannot be made thin; making it thin will sacrifice heat dissipation, performance, battery life, and key feel, which is harmful in every way.

## Steam, Steel, and Infinite Minds

![Steam, Steel, and Infinite Minds](../_images/14%20玄光周刊-当%20AI%20开始记住你的一切，这是我的使用策略-1774005263682.webp)

🔗 https://www.notion.com/zh-cn/blog/steam-steel-and-infinite-minds-ai

This is an article published by Notion's CEO at the end of last year. He uses the 'post-window' metaphor to remind us: don't treat AI merely as a smarter search box, just like early movies were just 'moving stage plays'. The real transformation lies in **reconstructing workflows**—letting AI agents handle fragmented tasks, humans upgrade from pedaling bicycles to driving cars, or even managing fleets.

Re-reading this article over the weekend, I have to sigh that within 3 months, the 'infinite minds' he talked about have already shown visible progress and implementation of AI integrating into workflows across various fields.
